Big Data

Author: Harry

Date: March 15, 2023

Big data refers to extremely large datasets that cannot be easily managed, processed, or analyzed using traditional data processing tools and techniques. The term "big data" is often associated with the three Vs: volume, velocity, and variety. Big data is characterized by its high volume, often in the terabytes or petabytes; its high velocity, which means it is generated and processed quickly; and its high variety, which refers to the diverse types of data, including structured, unstructured, and semi-structured data. The emergence of big data has led to the development of new technologies and tools, such as Hadoop and Spark, that enable organizations to store, manage, and process large volumes of data. These tools are designed to handle the complexity and scale of big data, and they often involve distributed computing and parallel processing.
